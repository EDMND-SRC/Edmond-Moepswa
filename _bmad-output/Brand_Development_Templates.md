## Brief Template

```
PROJECT BRIEF — [Client Name]

Date:
Prepared by:
Signed off by (client):

1. PURPOSE
Why does this brand exist beyond generating revenue? What would be lost if it ceased to exist?

2. AUDIENCE
Who is this brand for? Defined with precision, not demographic generalities. Name a specific person. What does their day look like? What are they worried about?

3. PROBLEM
What problem does this brand solve that competitors do not solve, or solve differently?

4. VALUES
What are the three to five non-negotiable principles that govern how this organisation operates? These are not aspirational — they must already be true.

5. SUCCESS
What does success look like in 12 months? In 5 years? What are the measurable signals?

6. EXCLUSIONS
What must this brand never be associated with? What should it never feel like or sound like?

7. COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE
Name three brands outside this category that you admire. Why?
Name three inside the category. Why?

8. PRIMARY COMMUNICATION
If the brand could only communicate one thing, what is it?

9. DECISION-MAKING
Who are the final sign-off stakeholders? How many people need to approve the work?

10. CONSTRAINTS
What legal, technical, cultural, or budgetary constraints exist that the design must navigate?

SIGN-OFF:
Client signature: _________________ Date: _______
Designer signature: _________________ Date: _______
```

<a name="interview"></a>

## Stakeholder Interview Template

```
STAKEHOLDER INTERVIEW — [Client Name] — [Stakeholder Name, Title]

Date:
Duration:
Interviewer:

1. Tell me about this organisation from your personal perspective — not the official narrative. What does this organisation do that no one else does?

2. Who is your most valuable customer? What do they say when they recommend you to someone else? Use their exact words.

3. What do you do better than anyone else? Give me your honest answer, not the marketing version.

4. What do your competitors do well that you do not do yet?

5. What parts of your current brand make you uncomfortable? What has always felt wrong?

6. If you were starting this organisation from zero today, what would you name it? Why?

7. What does this brand need to communicate to earn trust from a first-time buyer in under 10 seconds?

8. What is the single most important thing this project must achieve?

9. What would a failed project look like?

NOTES / KEY QUOTES:

AGREEMENTS WITH OTHER STAKEHOLDERS:

DISAGREEMENTS WITH OTHER STAKEHOLDERS:
```

<a name="positioning"></a>

## Positioning Statement Template

```
POSITIONING STATEMENT — [Client Name]

For [target audience] who [specific need or problem],
[brand name] is the [category definition]
that [primary differentiating benefit]
because [reason to believe].

EVALUATION:

[ ] Is it specific to this brand, or could any competitor use it?
[ ] Does it create a distinctive association?
[ ] Does it communicate the brand's primary positioning?
[ ] Can it survive for 10+ years without feeling dated?
[ ] Does it translate meaningfully across languages and cultures?

POSITIONING ANTI-PATTERNS CHECK:

[ ] No attribute parity ("innovative, customer-focused, high-quality")
[ ] No category defaults ("the leading provider of X")
[ ] No audience vagueness ("for businesses of all sizes")
[ ] No aspirational washing ("we believe in a better tomorrow")
```

## Client Presentation Outline

```
CLIENT PRESENTATION — [Phase Name] — [Client Name]

Date:
Duration:
Attendees:

1. CONTEXT (2 min)
[Where we are in the process. What gate we're at. What's been completed since last session.]

2. RECAP (3 min)
[Brief reminder of what was agreed at the last gate. Why we're here today.]

3. PRESENTATION (15–30 min)
[Present the work. For each concept or deliverable: the strategic rationale, the execution, how it addresses the brief.]

4. DISCUSSION (10–15 min)
[Open floor for questions. Capture feedback. Do not defend — listen.]

5. DECISION (5 min)
[Confirm the decision. If approved, move to the next phase. If revisions needed, confirm scope and timeline.]

6. NEXT STEPS (2 min)
[What happens next. Timeline. What the client needs to provide or do.]

[ ] Send presentation deck within 24 hours
[ ] Document feedback and decisions
[ ] Update project timeline if needed
[ ] Schedule next gate review
```
