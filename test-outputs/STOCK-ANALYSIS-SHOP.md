## 1. Thesis Outline

**Core Thesis:** Shopify remains the dominant infrastructure layer for global e-commerce. For a developer charging P5,000 for a Storefront integration, pinning services to the Shopify ecosystem provides extremely high mid-term safety due to its deep moats in B2B enterprise (Shopify Plus) and high switching costs for merchants.

## 2. Fundamental Diagnostics

### Ecosystem Strength (Developer Perspective)
- **The Tollbridge Model:** Shopify does not just charge SaaS fees; it acts as a tax on GMV via Shopify Payments. This ensures their revenue scales proportionally with their merchants' success, providing capital to continually reinvest in developer APIs (GraphQL, Hydrogen, Checkout Extensibility).
- **Vendor Lock-in (Neutral for Developers):** Once a merchant scales on Shopify, the cost of migration to Magento/Adobe or custom architecture is prohibitively high. This ensures the ongoing relevance of Shopify developer retainers.

### Headwinds & Risks
- **Amazon's "Buy with Prime":** Continues to challenge Shopify's checkout dominance. However, Shopify's logistics divestment (Deliverr) proved management is willing to ruthlessly correct strategic errors to protect margins.
- **Micro-Merchant Churn:** Low-tier drop-shippers churn rapidly.
  - *Strategic takeaway for service provider:* Do not target Tier A (P5,000+) Shopify builds toward micro-merchants or drop-shippers. Focus exclusively on established SMEs looking to migrate or scale.
