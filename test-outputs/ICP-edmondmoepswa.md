# Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) & Sales Targeting

**Business:** Edmond Moepswa (Digital Systems & Web Development)
**Core Offerings:** High-ticket web design (P13k-P19.5k), Web Apps (P25k-P48k), Workflow Automation (P2.5k-P6k+)

---

## The Target Prototype: "The Professional Services SME"

### Company Profile
- **Location:** Gaborone, Botswana (Primary) / SADC Region
- **Industry:** Legal, Financial Services, Real Estate, Medical/Specialist Clinics, B2B Consulting
- **Company Size:** 5-25 employees
- **Revenue Run-rate:** P2M+ annually

### The Decision Maker
- **Titles:** Owner, Managing Partner, Practice Manager, Founder
- **Age/Demographic:** 35-55, time-poor, values delegation, low tolerance for "tech jargon".
- **Psychographics:** They view their business as a premium service. They are frustrated that their current digital presence looks cheap and doesn't reflect the high value of the services they provide.

### Why They Need You
1. They are tired of "web designers" who build something pretty but structurally broken (no SEO, forms that go nowhere, vendor lock-in).
2. They are leaking revenue because manual processes (booking, quoting, onboarding) are handled via WhatsApp threads and spreadsheets.
3. They need systems built for "handover", not a retainer hostage situation.

---

## Disqualification Criteria (Red Flags)

**Do NOT target or immediately disqualify leads who:**
- Ask "Can you do it for P2,000?" (Your floor for a real Multi-Page presence is P13k).
- Don't have a clear business model or are "pre-revenue idea" startups.
- Want to dictate the tech stack despite having no technical background.
- Drag out the initial discovery call scheduling.

---

## Where to Find Them (Lead Sourcing)
- **LinkedIn:** Search "Managing Partner" OR "Director" AND "Botswana".
- **Local Directories:** High-end service directories, Botswana Law Society registry, Real Estate Institute listings.
- **Trigger Events:** Companies recently moving to new offices, launching new service lines, or running poorly structured Facebook Ads (indicating they have budget but bad execution).
