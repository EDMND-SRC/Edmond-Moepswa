# Marketing & Website Audit Report

**Target:** edmondmoepswa.com
**Date:** 2026-04-15
**Focus:** Copywriting, Funnel Architecture, Value Proposition

---

## 1. Hero & Value Proposition

### Current State
*Hero Copy:* "A systems thinker who understands P&Ls, risk, and production code."

### Analysis
- **Strengths:** Very strong positioning. It immediately differentiates you from standard "web designers" by framing you as a business operations partner. "Production code" + "P&Ls" speaks directly to founders and agency owners who care about ROI, not just aesthetics.
- **Weaknesses:** It's slightly abstract. A visitor looking for a "website builder" might not immediately realize that a "systems thinker" solves their exact problem until they scroll down. 

### Recommendation
**Add a definitive sub-headline that bridges the abstract with the concrete.**
*Suggested Sub-headline:* "I design, build, and automate digital infrastructure for businesses—from high-converting websites to complex Web Apps and Make.com automations."

---

## 2. Service Architecture & Pricing Strategy

### Current State
Tiered pricing structure visible for all services:
- Web Design: Launch (P5k), Presence (P13k), Growth (P19.5k)
- Web Apps: Storefront (P2.5k), Boilerplate (P25k), Custom SaaS (P48k)
- Workflows: Foundation (P2.5k), Multi-Step (P6k), Enterprise (Custom)

### Analysis
- **Strengths:** Extreme transparency. In the B2B service space, providing clear baseline pricing builds immense trust and disqualifies low-budget leads instantly. The naming conventions (Launch, Presence, Growth) clearly map to business maturity stages.
- **Weaknesses:** The cognitive load is high. Six core services, each with 3 tiers = 18 different pricing variables. A confused buyer says no.

### Recommendation
**Implement a Guided Funnel.** 
Instead of forcing the user to self-diagnose across 18 tiers, implement a simple interactive quiz or branching path:
1. "I need a website to generate leads" -> Routes to Web Design
2. "I need to sell digital products" -> Routes to Web Apps
3. "I need to stop doing manual tasks" -> Routes to Workflow Automation

---

## 3. Trust Signals & Proof

### Current State
Deep, narrative-driven biography ("Edmond learned systems thinking the hard way...").

### Analysis
- **Strengths:** The autobiographical approach builds deep parasocial trust. Mentioning "Botswana boardrooms" and "Canberra hospitality" provides unique authority and grounds your expertise in real-world operational stress-testing.
- **Weaknesses:** Missing quantitative proof. While the narrative is strong, B2B buyers look for numbers.

### Recommendation
**Add a "System Impact" section.**
Inject specific metrics or case studies right after the biography. 
- *e.g., "Built a Cal.com + Make.com automation that saved an agency 14 hours per week."*
- *e.g., "Deployed a Next.js storefront that processed P50k in the first month with zero downtime."*

---

## 4. Call-to-Action (CTA) Architecture

### Current State
Cal.com embed integration for discovery calls.

### Analysis
- **Strengths:** Frictionless booking. Moving straight to calendar availability rather than a generic contact form significantly increases conversion rates for high-ticket consulting.
- **Weaknesses:** The jump from reading to booking a 30-minute call is a high-commitment action for cold traffic.

### Recommendation
**Introduce a Low-Friction Lead Magnet.**
Create a secondary, lower-commitment CTA for visitors who aren't ready to book a call yet. 
- *Suggested Offer:* "The 5 Automations Every Botswana SME Should Implement." (Collects email / WhatsApp).

---

## Final Strategy Summary

**Grade:** A-
Your site acts as an excellent filter for high-intent, high-quality clients by leveraging transparent pricing and deep narrative authority. To push conversion rates higher, reduce the cognitive load of service selection and add quantitative case studies to back up your narrative authority.
