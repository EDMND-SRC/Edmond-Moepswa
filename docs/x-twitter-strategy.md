## Overview

X (formerly Twitter) is a real-time public conversation platform where developers, designers, founders, and tech professionals build audience, share knowledge, and attract inbound opportunities through public visibility. For Edmond's practice, X serves as a brand-building and network-expansion channel — particularly for reaching international clients, the tech community, and the build-in-public audience.

For Edmond's practice, X serves as:

1. **Build-in-public visibility** — Sharing project progress, technical decisions, and lessons learned attracts followers who are potential clients or referral sources
2. **Tech community participation** — Engaging with the web development, design, and automation communities establishes credibility among peers
3. **International reach** — Unlike LinkedIn (which is professional-network focused), X has a global audience — useful for attracting Upwork-adjacent international clients
4. **Thought leadership** — Commentary on technology trends, African tech ecosystem, and digital transformation positions Edmond as a voice in the space

### 2. Content Strategy

#### Post Types and Cadence

| Post Type | Frequency | Purpose |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| Build-in-public | 2–3/week | "Working on [X] today. Here's what I'm solving..." with screenshot or MockupViews image |
| Technical insight | 1–2/week | "Most Botswana business websites fail this one Core Web Vital..." — specific, actionable |
| Project showcase | 1–2/month | Thread: "Here's how I built [X] for [client]" — problem → approach → result |
| Commentary | 1–2/week | Opinion on tech trends, African tech ecosystem, AI, digital transformation |
| Engagement (replies) | Daily | Reply thoughtfully to 5–10 posts from people in your network |

#### Thread Strategy (1–2/month)
Long-form threads are X's highest-engagement content format:
- "I built a complete client website in 8 days. Here's the exact process, tools, and costs 🧵"
- "5 workflow automations I've built for Botswana businesses that saved them 10+ hours/week 🧵"
- "Why your business needs a Google Business Profile (and how to set it up in 30 minutes) 🧵"

Threads establish expertise, get bookmarked, and attract followers.

#### Build-in-Public Posts
Short, regular updates on current work:
- "Spent 3 hours today debugging a Supabase RLS policy. Turned out I was using auth.uid() on a table that didn't have it enabled. The fix: 1 line. The lesson: always check your policies first."
- "Just deployed a restaurant's booking system. Next.js + Cal.com + HubSpot CRM. All free tiers. Total platform cost to client: $0/month."

These posts humanise the work and attract people who appreciate transparency.

### 3. Engagement Strategy

#### Who to Follow
- African tech founders and builders
- Web developers sharing their work
- UX designers and accessibility advocates
- Automation and no-code/low-code practitioners
- Botswana and Southern African business accounts
- Potential clients (operations managers, marketing directors at target companies)

#### Reply Strategy
The most effective way to grow on X is not posting — it's replying. Spend 15–20 minutes daily replying to posts from people you follow:
- Add a technical perspective they didn't mention
- Share a relevant experience
- Ask a clarifying question that advances the discussion
- Avoid generic replies ("Great post!", "Agree!") — they add no value and no one notices them

#### Lists for Efficient Consumption
Create X Lists to curate your feed:
- "Tech Builders" — developers and founders sharing their work
- "African Tech" — African technology ecosystem accounts
- "Botswana Business" — local business and industry accounts
- "Clients & Prospects" — decision-makers at target companies

This keeps your feed focused on valuable content, not algorithm noise.

### 4. International Client Attraction

#### Why X for International Reach
- The tech community on X is global — a post from Gaborone gets the same visibility as a post from San Francisco
- Build-in-public content attracts founders and startup operators who hire freelancers
- Technical insight posts get bookmarked and shared, extending reach beyond your followers

#### Positioning for International Audience
- Frame Botswana experience as a unique perspective: "Building for emerging markets teaches you things Silicon Valley doesn't"
- Share universal technical insights (performance, accessibility, automation) — these are relevant everywhere
- Use relevant hashtags sparingly (#buildinpublic, #webdev, #solopreneur) — X's algorithm no longer relies heavily on hashtags

## Quick-Win Implementations

### Priority 1: Profile Setup (30 min)
1. Write bio with clear value proposition and location
2. Upload professional profile photo
3. Generate and upload branded header via Google AI tools
4. Pin your strongest post or thread
5. Add personal website link

### Priority 2: First Thread (2 hours)
Write and publish your first long-form thread:
- Topic: Something you've done recently that others can learn from
- Format: Hook tweet → 5–10 supporting tweets → summary/CTA
- Include screenshots, MockupViews images, or code snippets
- Post during peak hours (8–10 AM CAT on Tuesday–Thursday)

### Priority 3: Daily Engagement Habit (15 min/day)
- Open X → check Lists (not the algorithmic feed)
- Reply thoughtfully to 5–10 posts from people you follow
- Post 1 original tweet (build-in-public, insight, or commentary)
- Bookmark useful posts for future reference or thread material

## Risks & Considerations

1. **Algorithm volatility:** X's algorithm is unpredictable. A post can get 50 views or 50,000 with no obvious reason. Don't optimise for virality — optimise for consistency and quality
2. **Toxic environment:** X can be a negative space. Avoid political commentary, hot takes for engagement, and arguments. Stay focused on your area of expertise
3. **Time sink:** It's easy to spend 2 hours scrolling. Use Lists to curate your feed and set a timer for engagement sessions (15–20 minutes, not open-ended)
4. **No direct ROI tracking:** Unlike LinkedIn (where you can track profile views to enquiries), X's path from follower to client is indirect. Measure inbound enquiries by source — if X generates even 1 client per quarter, it's worth the time investment
5. **Platform risk:** X's ownership, policies, and user base are in flux. Don't build exclusively on X. Use it as one channel alongside LinkedIn, personal website SEO, and freelance platforms

|-----------|----------|-----------------|
| Build-in-public visibility | 2–3 posts/week showing real work | Growing audience of peers and prospects |
| Technical authority | 1–2 threads/month | Bookmarked, shared content that compounds |
| International reach | Global tech community engagement | 1–3 inbound enquiries/month |

**Key insight:** X is the highest-reach, lowest-cost platform for building a global professional presence. Unlike LinkedIn (which targets specific decision-makers), X casts a wider net — reaching developers, founders, designers, and potential clients across the world. The strategy is not to post constantly — it's to post consistently (2–3 times/week), engage thoughtfully (15 min/day), and write 1–2 substantial threads per month that demonstrate real expertise. The build-in-public approach is particularly powerful for a developer in Botswana — the perspective of building for emerging markets with free-tier tools is genuinely interesting to a global audience.
