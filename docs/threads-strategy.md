## Overview

Threads is Meta's text-first social platform, linked to Instagram for account creation and cross-posting. With 400M+ users as of 2026, it has become a significant channel for professional content, brand building, and community engagement — particularly for audiences who find X too noisy or adversarial.

For Edmond's practice, Threads serves as:

1. **Lower-friction alternative to X** — Same type of content (technical insights, build-in-public, commentary) but in a calmer, more constructive environment
2. **Instagram-adjacent reach** — Content posted on Threads can be cross-posted to Instagram, doubling the audience with one effort
3. **Professional community** — Growing community of developers, designers, founders, and business professionals who prefer Threads' moderation and culture
4. **Content repurposing hub** — Threads posts can be sourced from LinkedIn articles and X threads — one piece of content, three platforms

### 2. Content Strategy

#### Post Types and Cadence

| Post Type | Frequency | Purpose |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| Build-in-public | 2–3/week | "Today I'm solving [X] for a client. Here's my approach..." |
| Technical tip | 1–2/week | Quick, actionable advice — "Your Google Business Profile is probably missing this one thing" |
| Project showcase | 1–2/month | MockupViews image + brief case study |
| Business commentary | 1/week | Commentary on Botswana business landscape, digital transformation, operational efficiency |
| Engagement (replies) | Daily | Reply thoughtfully to 5–10 posts from people you follow |

#### Content Differences from X
Threads' audience skews more conversational and less performative. Adjust accordingly:
- **Less:** Hot takes, technical flexing, thread hooks
- **More:** Genuine questions, behind-the-scenes, helpful tips, personal observations
- **Tone:** Conversational and warm — like talking to a colleague, not presenting at a conference

#### Cross-Posting Strategy
- Post natively on Threads first (algorithm favours native content)
- Enable cross-posting to Instagram for visual posts (MockupViews images, project showcases)
- Don't cross-post from X to Threads — the tone and audience are different enough that repurposing should be manual, not automatic

### 3. Engagement Strategy

#### Who to Follow
- African developers and designers
- Business and entrepreneurship accounts in Southern Africa
- Web development and design professionals
- People who post about automation, productivity, and small business
- Potential clients — operations managers, business owners, marketing professionals

#### Reply Strategy
Threads rewards genuine conversation:
- Ask follow-up questions on people's posts
- Share your experience related to their topic
- Offer a specific, actionable suggestion
- Avoid generic agreement — add something new to the conversation

#### Community Building
- Start conversations with questions: "What's the most common mistake you see small businesses make with their websites?"
- Share failures and lessons: "I spent 4 hours debugging a webhook today. The problem? A trailing slash. Here's how I'll prevent it next time."
- Celebrate others' wins: Genuinely congratulating others builds relationships that compound over time

## Quick-Win Implementations

### Priority 1: Account Setup and Cross-Posting (15 min)
1. Create Threads account (linked to Instagram Professional account)
2. Write bio with clear value proposition
3. Enable automatic cross-posting to Instagram
4. Follow 50–100 relevant accounts (developers, business professionals, African tech)
5. Post your first introduction thread

### Priority 2: Content Repurposing Pipeline (30 min/week)
Create a simple workflow:
```
LinkedIn article → extract key points → 3–4 Threads posts
X thread → adapt tone (less performative, more conversational) → Threads post
Project completion → MockupViews image → Threads post + cross-post to Instagram
```

### Priority 3: Daily Engagement (10–15 min/day)
- Open Threads → check followed accounts feed
- Reply thoughtfully to 3–5 posts
- Post 1 original post (tip, observation, or question)
- Check Instagram cross-post performance via Instagram Professional Dashboard

## Risks & Considerations

1. **Platform maturity:** Threads is still evolving. Features, algorithm, and audience behaviour may change significantly. Don't over-invest in platform-specific strategies that may become obsolete
2. **Lower B2B intent:** Unlike LinkedIn (where people are actively looking for professional services), Threads users are generally in a social/consumption mindset. Inbound enquiries will be fewer than LinkedIn but the relationship-building value is real
3. **Instagram dependency:** Threads requires an Instagram account. If you don't use Instagram, Threads is less valuable. The cross-posting benefit only works if you maintain an active Instagram presence
4. **Discoverability:** Threads' search and discovery features are improving but still limited compared to X. Growing organically requires consistent posting and engagement, not just good content
5. **Content overlap with X:** Posting the same content on both platforms can feel redundant. Adjust tone and format for each platform's culture — Threads is more conversational, X is more performative

## Summary: Value to Practice

| Objective | Approach | Expected Outcome |
|-----------|----------|-----------------|
| Community building | Daily engagement, genuine replies | Growing network of peers and prospects |
| Content amplification | Cross-posting to Instagram | 2x reach for visual content |
| Brand awareness | 3–4 posts/week | Consistent presence in followers' feeds |
| Secondary lead source | Project showcases, tips | 0–2 enquiries/month |

**Key insight:** Threads is a supplementary channel — not a primary lead generator. Its value is in community building, content amplification (via Instagram cross-posting), and maintaining a presence on a growing platform with a more constructive culture than X. For Edmond's practice, the highest-value use case is cross-posting visual project showcases from Threads to Instagram, effectively getting two platform posts from one piece of content. Invest 10–15 minutes daily in engagement, post 3–4 times per week, and treat Threads as a long-term relationship-building channel rather than a direct revenue source.
