## Overview

Contra is a modern freelance platform that revolutionised the space by charging freelancers zero commission — revenue comes from client-side services (payment processing, premium client features). It combines portfolio, networking, and payments into a single platform, making it part professional network, part freelance marketplace, part invoicing tool.

For Edmond's practice, Contra serves as:

1. **Zero-commission freelance profile** — Keep 100% of earnings, unlike Upwork's 5% commission
2. **Portfolio-first presence** — Contra profiles are visually rich portfolio pages, not resume-style listings
3. **Professional networking** — Growing community of founders, operators, and creatives who prefer Contra's modern approach
4. **Invoicing and payments** — Built-in invoicing with client payment processing, contract management, and payment tracking

### 2. Service Positioning

#### Contra vs. Upwork Positioning
Contra's audience skews toward:
- Startup founders and operators
- Creative professionals
- Modern, design-conscious businesses
- People who value quality over cost

Position accordingly:
- Emphasise design quality and custom work (not "cheap")
- Highlight the finance and operations background (unusual for developers)
- Use professional, confident language — not "I'll do anything for a buck"

#### Service Packages on Contra
Unlike Upwork (where you respond to job postings), Contra lets you define services that clients can browse and book directly:
- Package your Foundation Build configurations (Launch, Presence, Growth) as Contra services
- Include the workflow automation Foundation Scenario as a service
- Package the GBP Quick Start as a service
- Package the Advisory Foundation Session as a service

This turns Contra into a self-service storefront — clients can browse, understand, and initiate engagement without a job posting.

## Risks & Considerations

1. **Smaller marketplace:** Contra has far fewer job postings than Upwork. Its value is not as a job board but as a portfolio platform and professional network. Don't expect the same volume of opportunities
2. **Client-side bias:** Contra's monetisation model means the platform optimises for client experience, not freelancer experience. This is generally positive (clients get good service) but means freelancer-specific features may be deprioritised
3. **Payment processing fees:** While Contra charges freelancers 0% commission, clients pay payment processing fees (typically 2.9% + $0.30). This is standard and comparable to Stripe/PayPal
4. **Platform maturity:** Contra is younger than Upwork and Fiverr. The user base is growing but still concentrated in tech, design, and creative industries — less presence in traditional business sectors
5. **No escrow for off-platform clients:** If you use Contra invoicing for clients you found independently, Contra's payment protection may not apply. Use it primarily for Contra-sourced clients
