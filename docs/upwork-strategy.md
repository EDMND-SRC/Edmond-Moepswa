## Overview

Upwork is the world's largest freelance marketplace, connecting businesses with independent professionals across all skill categories. For a Botswana-based web designer, developer, and automation specialist, Upwork provides access to international clients who are actively seeking the exact services Edmond offers — and who have budgets already allocated for the work.

For Edmond's practice, Upwork serves as:

1. **International client acquisition** — Access to businesses in the US, UK, Europe, and beyond actively searching for web designers, developers, and automation specialists
2. **Revenue diversification** — Reduces dependence on the Botswana market alone
3. **Portfolio building** — Early projects build Upwork reviews and Job Success Score, which attract higher-value clients
4. **Market validation** — Upwork's competitive bidding environment provides real-time pricing and positioning feedback

| Feature | Limit |
|---------|-------|
| Monthly cost | $0 |
| Connects per month | 10 free Connects |
| Commission on earnings | 5% (flat rate, reduced from previous tiered model in 2025) |
| Proposals per job | Varies — most jobs cost 4–16 Connects per proposal |
| Direct Contracts | 0% commission (no Upwork fee on clients you bring to the platform) |
| Profile | Full — portfolio, certifications, employment history, skills tests |
| Job feed | Full access to all posted jobs |
| Messaging | Only with clients who have invited you or after proposal acceptance |

**Freelancer Plus ($19.99/month):**
- 90 Connects/month (vs. 10 on Basic)
- 0% commission on Direct Contracts
- Competitor insights (see who else applied to a job)
- Activity analytics
- Keep earned badges and certifications visible

**Connect cost:** Each job posting requires 4–16 Connects to submit a proposal. Most mid-value jobs ($500–5,000) cost 8–12 Connects. With 10 free Connects/month, a Basic account can submit 1–2 proposals per month.

### 2. Proposal Strategy

#### Connect Economics (Basic Plan)
- 10 free Connects/month
- Most jobs cost 8–12 Connects per proposal
- **This means 1 proposal per month on the free plan**
- **Strategy:** Be extremely selective. Only propose to jobs where:
  1. The budget matches your pricing (don't underprice — it damages your rate history)
  2. The client has verified payment method
  3. The client has a history of hiring and good reviews from freelancers
  4. The job description is detailed (indicates a serious client)

#### Proposal Template (Adapt Per Job)
```
Hi [Client Name],

I read your brief for [specific project detail] and I can help.

Here's what I'd bring to this:
• [Specific relevant experience — "I recently built a similar booking system for a salon in Gaborone using Next.js and Cal.com"]
• [Technical approach — "I'd use Next.js for the frontend with a Supabase backend, which keeps your platform costs at $0/month"]
• [Business understanding — "Having managed P&L statements in hospitality, I understand why this matters for your operations"]

I'd like to understand [specific question about their requirements] before committing to a timeline and price. Are you available for a 15-minute call this week?

A bit about me: I'm a full-stack developer and workflow automation specialist based in Botswana. I've worked in finance, hospitality, and corporate risk — so I understand the business side, not just the code. Every project I deliver includes full documentation and handover.

Recent work: [link to portfolio or personal website]

Looking forward to learning more about [their project].

Best,
Edmond
```

#### Jobs to Target
- Web design and development ($1,000–10,000 range)
- Workflow automation and Make.com specialists
- SEO and technical SEO projects
- Next.js/React development
- Landing page design and development
- Business process automation

#### Jobs to Avoid
- Budget under $200 (race to the bottom)
- "Build me a clone of [major platform]" (unrealistic scope)
- Clients with no payment verification
- Clients with a history of not reviewing freelancers
- Vague job descriptions ("I need a website" with no details)

### 3. Building Reputation

#### First 3 Projects
The first 3 reviews are critical:
- Be willing to price competitively (not cheaply — competitively) for the first few projects
- Over-deliver on quality and communication
- Request a review at project completion: "If you're happy with the work, a review would help me a lot"
- Aim for 5-star reviews on the first 3 projects — this establishes a strong Job Success Score

#### Job Success Score (JSS)
- Calculated based on client satisfaction, earnings, and long-term relationships
- A JSS of 90%+ unlocks Top Rated status (after $1,000+ earned and 10+ hours worked)
- Top Rated freelancers get priority placement in search results and access to exclusive jobs

#### Direct Contracts
- If a client finds you on Upwork but you want to work outside the platform for future projects:
  - Upwork allows Direct Contracts with 0% commission (on Plus plan; 5% on Basic)
  - The client must have hired you on Upwork first
  - This is useful for retainer relationships that start on Upwork

## Risks & Considerations

1. **Limited Connects on free tier:** 10 Connects/month = 1 proposal. This is the biggest constraint. Upgrade to Plus ($19.99/month) as soon as you can justify it with even one additional project
2. **Race to the bottom:** Upwork has many low-cost providers from low-cost regions. Compete on quality, not price — your finance and operations background is a genuine differentiator that most developers don't have
3. **Payment protection:** Upwork's escrow protects both parties. Always use it — never work outside Upwork for the first engagement with a client
4. **Commission (5%):** On a $2,000 project, Upwork takes $100. This is the cost of client acquisition — compared to marketing, advertising, or proposal time, it's reasonable
5. **Time zone challenges:** Working with US/UK clients from Botswana (CAT = UTC+2) means some overlap is possible — CAT is 7 hours ahead of EST, 5 ahead of GMT. Morning CAT calls work for US afternoon, and late CAT calls work for US morning
6. **Account dependency:** Your Upwork reputation (reviews, JSS, earnings history) lives on the platform. If your account is suspended (rare but possible), you lose it all. Maintain your personal website and LinkedIn as independent channels
